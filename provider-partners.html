Partner With Us

Partner With Us to Expand Access to Care

Mental Wellness Connection is actively seeking partnerships with licensed behavioral health providers, community organizations, and referral partners throughout Arkansas.

Join Our Network
Partnership Benefits

What Providers Gain

Receive Referrals

Connect with new clients through our community referral and voucher programs.

Participate in Vouchers

Accept counseling vouchers as part of your practice's community care.

Support Underserved Populations

Extend care to individuals and communities that often go without it.

Collaborate With Community Organizations

Build relationships across Arkansas's behavioral health ecosystem.

Help Train Future Counselors

Supervise graduate interns and shape the next generation of clinicians.

Grow Your Practice's Impact

Be part of a statewide effort to improve access to outpatient mental health care.

Requirements

Who Can Partner With Us

  • Active, unrestricted Arkansas behavioral health license (LPC, LCSW, LMFT, Psychologist, or equivalent)
  • Current malpractice insurance
  • Willingness to accept referrals and/or vouchers based on availability
  • Agreement to Mental Wellness Connection's partnership terms and expectations
Referral Process

How Referrals Work

1

We match an individual's needs and location with your practice's availability.

2

We share relevant intake information with your consent workflow in place.

3

You schedule the client directly and provide care as you normally would.

Not a licensed clinician? Community organizations, churches, schools, and other referral partners are welcome to partner with us too — helping connect people who need support with our Get Help resources. Contact us to talk about a referral partnership.
Provider Expectations

What We Ask of Our Partners

Referral volume depends on your stated capacity, specialties, and location. You control how many clients you're able to accept at any time.

There is no cost to join as a provider partner. We ask for your good-faith participation in referrals, vouchers, or supervision, based on what fits your practice.

Yes. Providers can participate through referrals, voucher acceptance, intern supervision, or a combination — whatever best fits your practice.
